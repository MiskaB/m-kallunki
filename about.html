About Merja Kallunki

Breathing is remembering. Feeling is healing.

I’m Merja.

I come from the north. From the silence of Lapland, from snowy forests, from Sámi roots and reindeer paths. That land shaped me. It still does. Even though I’ve lived most of my adult life in Helsinki, my body belongs to the wild. To stillness. To breath. To the in-between spaces.

But my real journey didn’t start outside. It started within.

What cracked me open

  • Addiction cracked me open.
  • Violence taught me the shape of fear.
  • Holding my father as he passed taught me the rhythm of life and death.
  • And my children — They have been my mirrors. Their pain made me face my own. Made me scream. Made me surrender.

And in all of this... I discovered breath. The first one when we’re born. The last one when we leave. Everything in between is a chance to come back to the body.

“Pain that isn’t felt stays in the body. And the body always remembers.”

The body knows. That’s where I begin.

I don’t believe in fixing people. I believe in holding space. In listening. In feeling the edges of what’s been hidden — and gently letting it rise.

I’ve seen it again and again: trauma doesn’t just live in the mind. It lives in the breath. In muscles. In bones. In patterns we don’t even see.

So we don’t need to understand everything to heal. We need to breathe. To move. To be felt.

This is not performance. It’s presence. It’s process.

What I stand for

  • Embodiment over explanation. Not everything can be put into words — nor should it be.
  • Safety in presence. A healing space is one where you’re allowed to be exactly as you are.
  • Trauma as sacred ground. Our wounds are not shameful. They are entry points to transformation.
  • Breath as anchor. Always available. Always real. A way back to yourself.
  • Nature as teacher. Slow. Wild. Unapologetic. Like healing should be.

How I work

I don’t offer methods. I offer my lived experience. I don’t see myself as a healer.

I walk beside you. Sometimes I hold space. Sometimes I witness. Sometimes I remind you to feel your feet.

My offerings — breathwork, bodywork, rope — they all come from what I’ve lived through. From what I’ve needed myself. From what has brought me back, again and again, to the truth of my body.

People often say they feel something ancient in my work. Something honest. Something wild. That’s the part of you I want to meet.

Breath. Rope. Rhythm.

Rope found me later. And it spoke to something I already knew in my bones.

Shibari is not just aesthetic. It’s somatic. It’s breath in motion. It’s surrender with boundaries. It’s intimacy with yourself or another.

In my sessions and workshops, rope is a language. It says: I see you. You are held. You can let go.

Why I do this work

  • Because I’ve lived what I now offer.
  • Because I’ve seen how breath can soften what feels impossible.
  • Because I believe we all deserve to feel safe in our own bodies.

This is my way. This is my devotion. To truth. To presence. To return.
